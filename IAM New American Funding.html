How We Achieved 25% Risk Reduction

The Challenge: New American Funding faced significant unauthorized access risks due to legacy IAM systems and inconsistent access controls across the organization.

Our Strategic Approach:

  • Comprehensive Risk Assessment: Used advanced analytics tools (Splunk, Qualys) to identify and categorize all access-related vulnerabilities
  • Systematic Controls Implementation: Deployed layered security controls following NIST 800-53 guidelines
  • Continuous Monitoring: Established real-time monitoring systems to detect and respond to access anomalies
  • Process Standardization: Created consistent IAM procedures across all business units
  • Executive Reporting: Provided clear, actionable risk metrics to leadership using Power BI dashboards

Measurable Results: Through this systematic approach, we reduced unauthorized access incidents by 25%, while maintaining 100% compliance with regulatory requirements. This directly translated to reduced business risk exposure and enhanced customer trust.

How We Identified 200+ Vulnerabilities

Proactive Security Philosophy: Rather than waiting for security incidents to occur, we implemented a systematic vulnerability discovery and remediation program.

Our Discovery Methods:

  • Automated Scanning: Deployed Qualys vulnerability scanners across all systems to continuously identify security gaps
  • Manual Security Reviews: Conducted detailed access control audits to find configuration weaknesses
  • Threat Modeling: Analyzed potential attack paths through IAM systems to identify weak points
  • Compliance Gap Analysis: Compared current configurations against NIST, GDPR, and SOX requirements
  • Penetration Testing: Used controlled testing to validate real-world exploit potential

Impact: By identifying and fixing 200+ vulnerabilities before they could be exploited by attackers, we prevented potential security breaches and demonstrated due diligence to auditors and regulators. Each vulnerability fixed represented a potential attack vector eliminated.

How We Achieved 100% Audit Success Rate

Excellence Through Preparation: Our audit success comes from treating compliance as an ongoing process, not a periodic event.

Our Audit Strategy:

  • Continuous Documentation: Maintained real-time evidence collection for all IAM activities and changes
  • Proactive Control Testing: Regularly tested our security controls to ensure they worked as designed
  • Gap Remediation: Identified and fixed compliance gaps before auditors arrived
  • Staff Training: Ensured team members understood compliance requirements and could articulate our controls
  • Mock Audits: Conducted internal audits to identify potential issues before external reviews
  • Stakeholder Coordination: Worked closely with legal, IT, and business teams to ensure comprehensive compliance

Business Value: 100% audit success rate means no compliance violations, no regulatory fines, and enhanced stakeholder confidence. This directly supports business operations and growth by maintaining regulatory standing and customer trust.

NIST 800-53 Framework Implementation

Purpose: NIST 800-53 provides comprehensive security controls for federal information systems and organizations. We implemented this framework to establish robust, standardized security controls.

Key Annexes Used:

  • AC (Access Control): Implemented account management, access enforcement, and least privilege principles
  • AU (Audit and Accountability): Established comprehensive logging and monitoring capabilities
  • IA (Identification and Authentication): Deployed multi-factor authentication and identity verification
  • SC (System and Communications Protection): Secured data transmission and system boundaries

How It Achieved Our Goals: NIST 800-53 provided the foundational security architecture that enabled our 25% risk reduction. The framework's systematic approach to access controls directly supported our IAM objectives while ensuring comprehensive security coverage.

GDPR Compliance Framework

Purpose: The General Data Protection Regulation ensures privacy protection for personal data. Our implementation focused on access controls and data protection by design.

Key Articles Implemented:

  • Article 25 (Data Protection by Design): Built privacy controls into our IAM systems from the ground up
  • Article 32 (Security of Processing): Implemented appropriate technical measures to secure personal data
  • Article 30 (Records of Processing): Maintained detailed access logs and processing records
  • Article 35 (Privacy Impact Assessment): Conducted privacy assessments for all IAM changes

Achievement Impact: GDPR compliance protected us from potential fines up to 4% of annual revenue while building customer trust through demonstrated privacy protection. Our privacy-by-design approach in IAM systems directly supported business growth in privacy-conscious markets.

Sarbanes-Oxley (SOX) Compliance

Purpose: SOX requires internal controls over financial reporting. Our IAM implementation ensured proper access controls to financial systems and data.

Key Sections Addressed:

  • Section 302: Established executive certification processes for access control effectiveness
  • Section 404: Implemented and tested internal controls over financial system access
  • Section 409: Created real-time disclosure capabilities for access-related incidents

Business Value: SOX compliance prevented regulatory violations and supported investor confidence. Our systematic approach to financial system access controls directly enabled accurate financial reporting and supported the company's public market activities.

PCI-DSS Framework

Purpose: Payment Card Industry Data Security Standard protects cardholder data. Our implementation secured access to payment processing systems.

Key Requirements Implemented:

  • Requirement 7: Restricted access to cardholder data by business need-to-know
  • Requirement 8: Implemented unique user IDs and strong authentication for payment system access
  • Requirement 10: Established comprehensive logging and monitoring for all payment data access
  • Requirement 11: Conducted regular security testing of payment system controls

Achievement Impact: PCI-DSS compliance enabled secure payment processing, protecting both customer data and company reputation. This directly supported revenue growth by maintaining payment processor relationships and customer trust.

California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA)

Purpose: CCPA grants California consumers rights over their personal information. Our IAM implementation enabled consumer privacy rights management.

Key Provisions Addressed:

  • Right to Know: Implemented systems to track and report personal data access
  • Right to Delete: Created processes for secure data erasure while maintaining access logs
  • Right to Opt-Out: Built consent management into access control systems
  • Non-Discrimination: Ensured equal service delivery regardless of privacy choices

Business Value: CCPA compliance protected us from California state penalties while positioning us as a privacy-forward organization. This competitive advantage supported customer acquisition and retention in privacy-conscious markets.

NIST Cybersecurity Framework (CSF)

Purpose: The NIST CSF provides a policy framework for computer security guidance. We used this framework to structure our overall cybersecurity program.

Five Core Functions Implemented:

  • Identify: Cataloged all systems, assets, and data requiring access controls
  • Protect: Implemented access controls, awareness training, and protective technology
  • Detect: Deployed continuous monitoring and anomaly detection systems
  • Respond: Created incident response procedures for access violations
  • Recover: Established recovery procedures and lessons learned processes

Strategic Value: The CSF provided the overarching structure that unified all our security efforts. This holistic approach ensured comprehensive protection while providing a clear communication framework for executive reporting and stakeholder engagement.
